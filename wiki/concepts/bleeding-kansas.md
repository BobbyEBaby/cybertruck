# Bleeding Kansas (1854–1859)

The proxy war in Kansas Territory between pro-slavery and anti-slavery settlers, triggered by the Kansas-Nebraska Act of 1854, which repealed the Missouri Compromise's 36°30′ line and let territorial residents vote on whether Kansas would enter the Union as a slave state or a free state. The vote turned into a mutual invasion: pro-slavery Missourians crossing the border to vote and raid, and anti-slavery New Englanders moving to Kansas with rifles shipped in boxes labeled "Bibles."

Roughly 56 people were killed over five years. The most notorious incident on the anti-slavery side was the Pottawatomie Massacre of May 1856, in which [[figures/john-brown]] and his sons killed five pro-slavery settlers with broadswords. Bleeding Kansas was where Brown earned his reputation — and his willingness to use lethal force — before [[rebellions/harpers-ferry-raid-1859|Harpers Ferry]] in 1859.

## Why it matters to the channel's thesis

Bleeding Kansas is the reason the theme-framework's **Wars** rating for ep3 is 2 rather than 0. The Harpers Ferry raid was not Brown's first armed action; it was the next phase of a pseudo-war that had been running for five years. Viewers who don't know this history tend to read Harpers Ferry as a bolt from the blue. It was not. It was a continuation.
