# Philip I of Hesse

Landgrave of Hesse and one of the earliest and most important Protestant princes of the Holy Roman Empire. Military commander of the force that destroyed [[thomas-muntzer]]'s Thuringian peasant army at the [[../concepts/battle-of-frankenhausen|Battle of Frankenhausen]] on 15 May 1525.

## What we know

- Born in 1504; became Landgrave of Hesse at age 5 (regency until 1518).
- Converted to Lutheranism around 1524; one of the earliest German princes to do so.
- In May 1525, at age 20, he led his Landsknechts jointly with Duke George of Saxony to annihilate the peasant army under [[thomas-muntzer]] at Frankenhausen. ≈7,000 peasant dead versus reported Landsknecht losses of 6.
- Later a key organiser of the Schmalkaldic League, the Protestant princes' military alliance against Emperor [[charles-v]].
- Ended his career as a figure of the Reformation establishment — the exact kind of "reformist prince" the 1525 peasant rising had been, implicitly, asking not to exist.

## Significance

Philip of Hesse illustrates the political outcome that [[martin-luther]]'s [[../concepts/against-the-murderous-thieving-hordes-1525|May 1525 pamphlet]] helped cement: the Reformation survived by binding itself to princely authority, and that binding required the princes to be willing to slaughter peasants in the name of Protestant order. Philip was willing.
