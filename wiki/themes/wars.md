# Theme — Wars

One of the six recurring conditions. Wars covers **military overreach, veteran grievances, conscription, border losses, and the domestic pressures a long or failing war puts on the population**.

## How to rate intensity (0–3)

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **0** | No relevant war |
| **1** | A war is ongoing but not a direct contributor to the rebellion's grievances |
| **2** | The war is the proximate cause of the grievance (usually via war-related taxation or conscription) even if the war itself isn't what rebels are demanding to change |
| **3** | Rebels explicitly demand changes to the war itself — withdrawal, peace, or refusing to fight |

## What to look for

- **Is the war being lost or won?** A losing war that still demands tribute is the dangerous combination. A victorious war can sustain remarkable taxation; a losing one cannot.
- **Veterans returning with grievances.** Men who have fought and lost and come home to find their land taken or their wages frozen are rebellion accelerant.
- **War debt spilling into taxation.** The line between [[wars]] and [[debt]] is often the question of who ultimately pays for the fighting.

## Observations across rebellions

### [[../rebellions/peasants-revolt-1381]] — intensity **2/3**
The Hundred Years' War with France, in its fourth decade and going badly, was the proximate reason for the [[../concepts/poll-tax-1381]]. England was losing. The war was unpopular. But the rebellion itself was not *about* the war — the rebels did not demand withdrawal from France. They demanded the end of the tax, the end of villeinage, and the execution of traitors. So: war was the *cause* of the grievance but not the *content* of the demands. Rating 2.

## Pattern notes

- A rebellion rating 3 on wars is rare. Rebels are usually more concerned with the *symptoms* of war (taxes, conscription, veterans) than the war itself.
- Wars create demand for rebellion via [[debt]] — war-financing debt gets passed to the population as taxation, which creates the economic pressure that combines with [[government]] failure to produce the rebellion.
