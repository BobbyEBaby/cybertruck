# Theme — Environment

One of the six recurring conditions. Environment covers **famine, plague, weather shocks, crop failures, resource depletion — the non-human pressures that shape what humans can do**. This is often the deepest root cause: environment shapes economy, economy shapes government response, government response shapes the rebellion.

## How to rate intensity (0–3)

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **0** | Stable conditions, no environmental shock within living memory |
| **1** | Recent bad harvest or regional disease, but not central to the rebellion's origin |
| **2** | A significant environmental shock in the past decade that reshaped material conditions |
| **3** | A generational-scale shock (plague, famine, prolonged climate shift) that explains *why this rebellion happens now and not 30 years earlier or later* |

## What to look for

- **The shock doesn't have to be current.** The [[../concepts/black-death]] of 1348–1351 is the shadow over the [[../rebellions/peasants-revolt-1381]] — three decades later, but still the single biggest cause.
- **Demographic aftermath.** A plague-killed population creates labour shortages for a generation. A famine-killed population leaves land empty. These second-order effects are what rebels actually fight over.
- **Climate signals.** Cold decades, failed harvests over multiple years, the expansion or contraction of arable zones. These are slow but decisive.

## Observations across rebellions

### [[../rebellions/peasants-revolt-1381]] — intensity **3/3**
The [[../concepts/black-death]] is the shadow that falls across the whole story. Without the plague, there is no rebellion. The plague killed a third to a half of England's population between 1348 and 1351. The survivors' labour became suddenly more valuable. The [[../concepts/statute-of-labourers]] was the government's attempt to hold that against the market. The Peasants' Revolt was what happened when the population that benefited from the new labour scarcity met the population that was trying to deny them its benefits. Maximum intensity — without the environment, there is no 1381.

## Pattern notes

- Environment is the theme most likely to be **invisible** to the rebels themselves. A 14th-century peasant didn't know the Black Death had changed the labour market; they just knew their wages should be higher. The environment shows up in the rebellion's conditions, not its demands.
- High environment intensity + high economy intensity is the most common combination in pre-modern rebellions. The environment creates the shock; the economy translates it into a grievance.
- Environment-driven rebellions are among the most likely to **win structurally** (see [[../concepts/structural-vs-tactical-victory]]). You can crush the rebellion; you cannot bring back the dead population.
