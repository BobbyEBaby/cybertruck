# Christoph Schappeler

Swiss-born reformer and preacher at St Martin's Church in Memmingen. Generally credited with writing the preamble and theological justification of the [[../concepts/twelve-articles-1525|Twelve Articles of the Swabian Peasants]], with [[sebastian-lotzer]] drafting the body of the text in late February and early March 1525.

## What we know

- Born in St Gallen around 1472; studied theology and began preaching Reformation doctrine in Memmingen from 1513 onward.
- A member of the reformist circle that made Memmingen a hub of south German evangelical activity in the early 1520s.
- Collaborated with [[sebastian-lotzer]] on the [[../concepts/twelve-articles-1525|Twelve Articles]], supplying the Scripture citations and the theological frame that turned a bundle of grievance lists into a coherent Christian-justified political manifesto.
- After the revolt was crushed he fled Memmingen for his native Switzerland, where he lived out the rest of his life in St Gallen. He died in 1551.

## Significance

Schappeler is the pattern of "reform-minded cleric who helps working people articulate their grievances in the language of the dominant ideology." The Twelve Articles take demands that could be dismissed as self-interested peasant complaining and reframe them as Scripture-backed Christian duty — a move that is only possible with a trained theologian's help.

Unlike [[thomas-muntzer]], Schappeler did not advocate armed revolt. The Articles themselves are non-violent. But the princes did not draw that distinction.
