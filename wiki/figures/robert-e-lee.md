# Robert E. Lee (1807–1870)

U.S. Army career officer. Appears in this wiki for a single 1859 cameo: commanding the Marine detachment that stormed the engine house at [[rebellions/harpers-ferry-raid-1859|Harpers Ferry]] and captured [[figures/john-brown]] on the morning of October 18, 1859. At the time Lee was a forty-two-year-old colonel on leave from his post, summoned at short notice because he happened to be at Arlington when the alert came.

Two years later, Lee resigned his U.S. commission to command Virginia's forces, and eventually the Confederate Army of Northern Virginia. He surrendered at Appomattox Court House in April 1865.

The fact that the officer who ended Brown's raid would later lead the armies Brown's raid arguably precipitated is one of the cleaner historical ironies in the American record. Worth naming, not dwelling on.
