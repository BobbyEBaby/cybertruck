## Figures

- [[figures/wat-tyler]] — Kent roofer. Led the 1381 Peasants' Revolt. Murdered at Smithfield by [[figures/william-walworth]].
- [[figures/richard-ii]] — King of England 1377–1399. 14 years old during the Peasants' Revolt. Reigned by the council of his uncle [[figures/john-of-gaunt]].
- [[figures/william-walworth]] — Lord Mayor of London 1381. Killed [[figures/wat-tyler]] at Smithfield.
- [[figures/simon-sudbury]] — Archbishop of Canterbury and Lord Chancellor of England. Beheaded by rebels on Tower Hill, June 1381.
- [[figures/john-of-gaunt]] — Uncle of [[figures/richard-ii]]. Effective ruler of England in 1381. Target of the rebels' hatred; his Savoy Palace was burned.

## Concepts

- [[concepts/structural-vs-tactical-victory]] — the channel's central thesis: rebellions rarely win in their own moment, but the conditions that cause them almost always win in the century that follows.
- [[concepts/black-death]] — the 1348–1351 plague that killed ~40% of England's population and created the labour-market conditions for the 1381 revolt.
- [[concepts/statute-of-labourers]] — the 1351 English law that froze wages at pre-plague levels, the primary economic grievance of the Peasants' Revolt.
- [[concepts/poll-tax-1381]] — the third successive poll tax, imposed at 12 pence per head regardless of income, the proximate trigger of the Peasants' Revolt.
- [[concepts/villeinage]] — the legal status of serfs bound to a manor. Formally preserved by the crown after 1381; quietly dead within 100 years.
