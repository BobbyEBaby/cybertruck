# Charles V

Holy Roman Emperor from 1519, King of Spain from 1516, and the most powerful ruler in Europe at the outbreak of the [[../rebellions/german-peasants-war-1524]]. Notably, **he was not in Germany when it happened.**

## What we know

- Ruled an empire that stretched from Spain and the Low Countries across Germany, Austria, Italy, and the New World.
- At the time of the Peasants' War (1524–1525), Charles was in Spain and fully engaged in the Italian Wars against King Francis I of France. The decisive Battle of Pavia — where Charles's forces captured Francis I — was fought on 24 February 1525, just as the peasant risings in Swabia were gathering strength.
- Did not personally intervene in the German rising. Left the suppression to regional princes ([[philip-of-hesse]], Duke George of Saxony, the Swabian League).
- After the war his focus remained on France and the Ottomans; the internal politics of German serfdom were delegated to the princes, who made their own arrangements with the peasants on their own lands.

## Significance

Charles's absence is why the [[../themes/royalty]] theme rating for 1524–25 is low (1). Unlike [[richard-ii]] in 1381, who was physically in London and physically at Smithfield, Charles was a continent away from 1525's climax. The rebels never named him as enemy. The enemies were the local princes.

This is an important pattern to notice: in multi-layered feudal systems, the "ruler" the rebels blame is almost never the top ruler. It is the layer directly above them. A distant emperor can be invisible to the grievances while the prince-bishop down the road is maximally present.
