## Rough beat map (for the agent writing the full script)
- **Hook (0:00–0:20):** "In July 1518, a woman in Strasbourg started dancing in the street. She did not stop for days. By the end of the month, 400 people had joined her — and dozens were dead."
- **Context (0:20–1:30):** Strasbourg in 1518. Famine years. Religious tension. Pre-Reformation anxiety. Authorities who believed physical dancing could cure the "dancing mania."
- **The story, beat 1 (1:30–3:00):** Frau Troffea, the first dancer. Public incredulity → crowd gathering → more joining.
- **The story, beat 2 (3:00–4:30):** The authorities' disastrous response: they built a stage, hired musicians, paid dancers to join. They thought the way out was *through*. It wasn't. People began collapsing and dying from heart attacks, exhaustion, strokes.
- **The story, beat 3 (4:30–5:30):** How it ended. Pilgrimage to a shrine of Saint Vitus. The plague tapering off for reasons still debated: ergot poisoning, mass psychogenic illness, or a combination.
- **Why it matters (5:30–6:30):** Mass hysteria is real. Humans are capable of sincere, sustained, deadly group behavior when the social and physical conditions align. Relevant now in ways viewers will recognize without being told.
